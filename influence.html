Influence

See how Sơn Tùng M-TP reshaped V-Pop, inspired a new generation, and bridged Vietnam with the world

Impact on the Vietnamese Music Industry

Son Tung M-TP transformed the Vietnamese music industry by founding his own label, M-TP Entertainment, which gave him full creative control and set a new precedent for artist-led careers in V-Pop. Through this move, he proved that independent artists could achieve mainstream success while redefining how music was produced, marketed, and shared in Vietnam. His success inspired a shift in the industry, encouraging more artists to pursue self-managed paths and raising the overall standard for professionalism and innovation in V-Pop.

Redefining V-Pop’s Sound and Image

Sơn Tùng M-TP broke away from the traditional ballad-heavy style that had long dominated Vietnamese pop by fusing pop with hip-hop, R&B, and electronic elements. Songs like Em Của Ngày Hôm Qua and Chúng Ta Không Thuộc Về Nhau showcased catchy hooks, layered beats, and Western-inspired production that felt fresh to Vietnamese audiences. His experimentation gave V-Pop a modern identity, proving it could compete sonically with global pop trends.

Equally important was his visual presentation. Sơn Tùng embraced bold fashion choices, from streetwear and oversized silhouettes to high-fashion looks, often drawing comparisons to international icons like G-Dragon. His cinematic music videos, polished choreography, and striking aesthetics elevated V-Pop’s image beyond local expectations. By combining sound and style, he reshaped how audiences perceived Vietnamese pop as a full cultural experience.

Redefining V-Pop’s Sound and Image

Son Tung M-TP’s rise became a model for younger artists who saw him as proof that creativity and persistence could break barriers in V-Pop. His success showed that it was possible to build a career outside of traditional industry structures, inspiring a new generation to experiment with sound, image, and identity.

Artists such as Đức Phúc, Erik, Soobin Hoàng Sơn, Amee, Kay Trần, and Hiền Hồ represent this wave of talent shaped by his influence. Đức Phúc and Erik embraced polished pop ballads and modern visuals, while Soobin Hoàng Sơn leaned into R&B and hip-hop, echoing Son Tung’s genre-blending approach. Amee and Hiền Hồ brought youthful energy and bold fashion into their music, reflecting the stylistic freedom he popularized. Kay Trần, who joined M-TP Entertainment, stands as a direct example of Son Tung’s mentorship and leadership in guiding emerging talent.

Fashion and Style Trends

Son Tung M-TP established himself as a fashion leader in Vietnam by constantly reinventing his look and integrating style into his artistry. He moved fluidly between streetwear, oversized silhouettes, and luxury fashion, often drawing comparisons to international icons like G-Dragon. His stage outfits, music video styling, and public appearances created trends that fans and fellow performers quickly adopted.

Haircuts, accessories, and bold color choices became part of his signature, influencing how younger audiences expressed themselves. Designers and stylists began to see him as a cultural touchpoint, using his image to showcase modern Vietnamese fashion. By making style inseparable from his music, Son Tung elevated V-Pop’s visual identity and inspired a generation to view fashion as a powerful extension of creativity.

Bridging Local and Global Audiences

Son Tung M-TP played a key role in bringing Vietnamese pop culture to international attention. His ability to adapt global influences into his music and visuals made V-Pop more accessible to audiences outside Vietnam. Songs like Lạc Trôi and Hãy Trao Cho Anh gained millions of views on YouTube, proving that Vietnamese music could resonate worldwide.

He also collaborated with international producers and artists, most notably Snoop Dogg in Hãy Trao Cho Anh, which marked a milestone for V-Pop’s global reach. His strong online presence, including social media engagement and streaming platforms, allowed him to connect directly with fans across Asia, Europe, and North America. By bridging local traditions with global trends, Son Tung positioned himself as a cultural ambassador who expanded the visibility of Vietnamese music on the world stage.

Family Legacy

Son Tung M-TP’s influence extended beyond the industry to his own family, inspiring his younger brother, Mono, to pursue a career in music. Mono followed a similar path into V-Pop but made a deliberate choice to build his reputation without relying on his brother’s fame. His debut projects showcased a distinct sound and image, proving his commitment to carving out an identity that was uniquely his own.

Mono’s independence highlights the strength of Son Tung’s legacy. Rather than overshadowing his brother, Son Tung’s success created space for Mono to thrive while still encouraging individuality. Together, they represent two different approaches to artistry within the same family, showing how Son Tung’s impact was powerful enough to inspire but flexible enough to allow others to define their own creative journeys.
